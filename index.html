Battery vs. Generator

The honest comparison—you decide what matters

Feature Battery Backup Generator What This Means
Noise Silent 70+ dB (loud) Batteries win if you value peace and quiet
Maintenance Minimal Oil changes, testing Batteries: set-and-forget. Generators: annual service
Startup Time Instant 15-30 seconds Both fast enough for most needs
Runtime 8-24 hours Unlimited (with fuel) Generators win for multi-day outages
Upfront Cost $13k-17k $5k-15k Generators cheaper initially
10-Year Total Cost $15k-30k $12k-25k Generators still cheaper (maintenance included)
Fuel/Energy Charges from grid Propane/gas required Batteries work if grid recovers; generators need fuel delivery
Tax Credits 30% federal (if solar) None With incentives, battery cost drops to $10k-21k

Battery Backup Makes Sense If:

  • ✓ You want silent, automatic backup
  • ✓ Outages are typically 1-2 days
  • ✓ You're adding solar (maximizes incentives)
  • ✓ You value low maintenance
  • ✓ HOA/neighborhood restricts generators

Generator Makes Sense If:

  • ✓ You need multi-day backup capacity
  • ✓ Lower upfront cost is priority
  • ✓ You don't mind noise/maintenance
  • ✓ You can store propane/gas safely
  • ✓ You're not adding solar

Our take: Batteries + solar make sense for most homeowners we work with—but generators are perfectly valid if your priorities are different. Let's run your specific numbers.
